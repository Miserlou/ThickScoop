thick scoop records

call for submissions

For the past few years, there has been a wonderful noise skirting around the edges of different bass music scenes. It's an irresistable groove, pleasing to the ears and belly, familiar yet foreign, rowdy yet smooth. It's something that isn't quite drum and bass and isn't quite trap, but it makes speakerboxes rattle and we fucking love it.

We are inviting producers to submit tracks to a new label imprint, Thick Scoop, which intends to catalyse and crystalize this sound.

Rather than being vague and varied in our output, we are instead providing a brief which tilts towards a hazy sketch of what we are hoping to hear.

brief

Sub Focused Music For Soundsystem Operators

This is music designed to be played on real soundsystems - the kind with 18" speakers, scooped cabinets, kickbins and horns. The primary focus of the track should be in the sub-bass. As such, the sub should be groovy and interesting - modulated in frequency and amplitude - not just a repetative subkick or straight 808. Real systems are properly amplified as well, so keep in the transients and skip the sausages.

Triplet Halftime

We're looking for bouncy, dancable bass music which fits into both Drum and Bass and Trap sets. This lands in at between 160-175 beats per minute, halved to 80-85bpm, dotted to 110bpm. Maybe think of it as triplet halftime. Say no to robotic quantization and overused sample packs, say yes to jangly metallic percussion, organic atmospheres and wobbly synths on the up beat.

Dedicated to the Party Vibe

There's already enough producers making tracks that are only designed to impress other producers. Instead, we're looking for tracks that keep it smooth and dancable, so please no overharsh midbass that clears the dancefloor. Err on the mellow and keep it groovy.

DJ Friendly

This is music for intended for live reproduction, so make life easy for the DJs. Provide intros and outros that are easy to mix into, let the beat ride, and make tracks that build and release energy without killing the dance floor with long swooshy parts in the middle.

Of course, rules are made to be broken - though we hope that these contraints breed creativity.
